George Mekhail

Co-Author, "Thank God for Bitcoin"

George Mekhail is the co-author of Thank God for Bitcoin, a book exploring the intersection of faith, money, and biblical stewardship. He serves as Managing Director of Bitcoin for Corporations at BTC Inc., where he helps companies integrate Bitcoin into their strategic operations.

With a rich background in ministry, leadership, and family dynamics, George brings a unique perspective to conversations about money and morality. His insights into Christianity and financial stewardship are rooted in a deep understanding of the faith, yet presented in a way that's accessible and relevant to individuals from all walks of life. George's perspective on spirituality isn't just about beliefs—it's about how those beliefs can be lived out in daily life, impacting personal growth and community well-being.

A father of two, George is also the author of I Am Not Your Bruh, a book exploring his philosophy on Sound Parenting. He co-organizes the Arizona Bitcoin Network and has spoken at multiple Bitcoin conferences aiming to demystify the complex concepts in our rapidly changing world while working to make them accessible to a wider audience.

About "Thank God for Bitcoin"

This transformative work examines how Bitcoin aligns with timeless principles of honest money, freedom, and human dignity. The book brings together voices from theology, economics, and technology to explore Bitcoin's role in promoting justice and stewardship.

What to Expect from This Talk

  • • Why money is a deeply spiritual topic that the Bible addresses over 2,000 times
  • • How "honest weights and measures" (Proverbs 11:1) connects to the money in your pocket today
  • • The biblical case against debt-based money and why it matters for your future
  • • How broken money systems incentivize materialism, debt slavery, and short-term thinking
  • • Why Bitcoin's fixed supply aligns with biblical principles of stewardship and honest money
  • • Practical ways to think about money, saving, and planning that honor God
  • • Q&A session to dive deeper into your questions
